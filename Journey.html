Countries

Haiti

Haiti is a small country off the Caribbean coast. It is the poorest country in the Western hemisphere. It has a population of approximately 10.9 million people. The official language is French. Haiti is still recovering from the life-altering earthquake that hit on January 12, 2010. The country is still try to recover from this natural disaster. Haiti is experiencing a spiritual revival like it has never seen before. Communities are turning to churches and pastors for leadership and help.



Dominican Republic

The Dominican Republic is a small country that borders Haiti. It was discovered by Christopher Columbus in 1492. After the devastating earthquake that hit in 2010, the country became a refuge for thousands of people who lost their home in the earthquake. The nation's economy is highly dependent on tourism. Policital corruption and the absence of a middle-class are two major issues present in the country.



South Africa

South Africa is located at the southern tip of Africa. It is one of the wealthiest countries in the continent, but most of the population outside of the big cities are poor. In 1994, apartheid (racial separation) was put to an end legally. Although the country has progressed towards greater political equality and economic stability, it sill faces challenges such as rising crime rates, ethnic tensions, and great disparities in housing and educational opportunities. This country also struggles with high rates of HIV/AIDS.



Botswana

Botswana borders Swaziland, and is one of the most sparesly populated countries on earth. It is rich with a variety of wildlife including many species of mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish. It also has the third highest HIV rate in the world. The country struggles with drought and desertification. Around 70% of the population professes Christianity, but 20% of the country does not practice any religion.



Zambia

Zambia is a landlocked country in Africa situated on a high plateau. The majority of the country's labor force works in the agriculture business. Zambia has a vast land and natural resourc base, but only about one-sixth of the country's arable land is under cultiviation. With the country near the bottom of the United Nations Human Development Index, they suffer from poverty, short life expectancy, malnourishment, lack of clean water, and the AIDS crisis.



India

India occupies most of Southern Asia and contains nearly a sixth of the world's population. It has one of the most highly diversified economies in the world, but because of its enormous population, it is one of the poorest countries on earth. It is home to 29 different languages. The religions practiced in India are Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and old tribal worship. There is a highly segregated rich and poor class. Due to the ancient caste system, crimes of social injustic, discrimination, forced prostitution, and human trafficking occur daily.



Nepal

Nepal is a country in Asia wedged between two giants, India and China. It seeks to keep a balance between the two countries in its foreign policy and ultimately to remain independent. It is home to Mount Everest, the world's tallest mountain. Nepal contains some of the most rugged mountain terrain in the world. Approximately 75% of the country is covered by mountains. It is home to Mount Everest which is the tallest mountain in the world. Nepal is also one of the least developed nations in the world.



Romania

Romania is approximately one-third moutinas, one-third forest, and the the remainder bade up of hils and plains. It contains fertile land for growing crops and raising livestock. The lore of Dracula brings thousands of tourists to explore the history and beauty of Romania. It was formerly part of the Soviet Union and has been growing economically ever since. The country is quickly rising out of its former impovereished state, but there are still many orphans and widos who struggle for food and a hope for the future.



Albania

Albania is a small country located in southern Europe and borders the Adriatic Sea. It has long served as a bridgehead for various nations seeking conquest abroad. It declared independence in 1912 but was occupied by both Germany and Italy during World war II. Not long after the fall of the Soviet Union, conflict on the Balkan Peninsula tore families apart. Due to centuries of Ottoman Rule, Albania is predominantly a Muslim country. However, as a result of strict enforcement of atheism during the communist regime, today most of Albanians claim religious groups in name only and practice largelky secular lifestyles.



Bulgaria

Bulgaria was formerly part of the Eastern Bloc and remained under socialism until 1990. Today it is a part of the European Union. The majority of religious Bulgarians are followers of the Bulgarian Orthodox Church while the minority religious groups are Muslims, Protestants, Catholics, and Jews. It has the second highest number of Mensa members in the world. It is also a primary destination point for human trafficking in Eastern Europe.



Serbia

Serbia is a county in the west-central Balkans. For most of the 20th century, it was a part of Communist Yugoslavia. The Balkan Peninsula has a long history of ethnic tensions due to reorgranized broders didn't always match the people living within the nation. While many ethnic Servs live in Albania and other nearby countries, many claim another country as home. Social injustices onboth sides have led to organized crime and human trafficking in the country.
